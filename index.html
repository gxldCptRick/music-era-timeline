Medival Era

500-1400

x

Hildegard von Bingen also known as saint hildegard and sibyl of the rhine was a german nun and also considered the founder of the scientific natural history, the research and study of organisms, in germany. She was born around the year 1098, although an exact date is uncertain. Hildegard was having visions from an early age and thus was given to the church by her parents. While serving in the church she was writing church music and wrote many songs including Alleluia, o virga mediatrix. Hildegard von Bingen died the 17th of september in 1179.

Alleluia, o virga mediatrix was a monophonic piece that was gregorian chant in the Choral Music Genre and has no rhythm because of the time it was created composers were not thinking of music in that kind of detail let alone the fact that they only had a limited way to be able to express their musical ideas.

Notre dame school of polyphony refers to a group of composers working at or near the notre dame cathedral in paris form about 1160 to 1250 along with the music they produced. One of the famous songs they made was called Gaude Maria Virgo - Victoria.

Gaude Maria Virgo was one of the first examples of organum and thus was one of the original uses of polyphony where they used more than one voice to sing the chant but they raised the singer 5 marks above the melody line. It of course was played in acapella, no instrumental accompaniment, almost all the music in this era.

x

Guillaume de Machaut was born around the 1300 and was a medieval french poet and composer. He was a part of the musical movement known as the ars nova or “new art” and that lead into better notation of the musical arts and thus more control over the way the music would sound to the composer. One of the songs that machaut wrote for the ars nova was Ma fin est mon commencement.

Ma fin est mon commencement was a polyphonic chanson in which if played either from beginning to end or end to beginning it sounded the same. It used three voices in a rondeau form.

Jacques arcadelt was a composer of the renaissance born in 1507 and died on october 14th, 1568. He was active in both Italy and France and principally known for the madrigals he wrote. One of his madrigals, Il bianco e dolce cigno, was a very popular madrigal of its time.

Il bianco e dolce cigno is an example of an italian madrigal. It is performed by amateurs in a cappella with four voices and it is mostly homophonic.

Tielman susato is a renaissance composer but not much is known about his early life and even his later life we assume that he was born around 1510 to 1515 but we are not certain of an exact date. His last known records were in 1570 so he must have died after that. He went around and printed music in the areas and once song he composed himself was called the 3 dances.

The 3 dances was a ronde or round dance and had a loud ensemble. It was in the binary form with both parts repeated and the harmony was consonant with full triads while the texture stayed mostly homophonic

Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina, born in 1525 and died in February of 1594, was an italian renaissance composer of sacred music and created many of the mass ordinary songs that are still sung to this day. His song Gloria, Pope Marcellus Mass is an example of one of his song still sung today.

Gloria, Pope Marcellus Mass is a sacred choral work from the mass ordinary. It is sung with a 6-part choir that originally was all male. It is Through-composed in two parts and has a mixture of Homorhythmic and Imitative polyphony.

Baroque Era

1600-1750

x

Henry Purcell was born in 10 of september of 1659 and died 21 November 1695. He is known as one of the greatest english born composers of all time and has composed many different pieces of music. One of his Operas Dido and Aeneas is what we are going to be discussing today.

In Dido and Aeneas i'm going to be talking specifically about Act III Dido’s Lament. This is the final act of the opera and it is sung by a soprano accompanied by a small orchestra with continuo. It is in the recitative and aria form in 2 parts over a ground bass.

x

Antonio Lucio Vivaldi was born in march 4 1678 and died on july 28 1741. He was an italian baroque composer and virtuoso violinist as well as a teacher and a cleric. He composed many instrumental concertos for the violin and many other instruments as well. One of his violin concertos was called the 4 seasons.

Within the 4 seasons concerto we will be talking about the first part spring. The spring concerto was a programmatic concerto for the violin and featured a solo violin accompanied by an orchestra. It used the ritornello form alternating with contrasting episodes. And was homophonic throughout the piece.

George Frideric Handel, born February 23 1685 and died April 14 1759, he was a german, later british, baroque composer. He spent the majority of his career in london and became well known for his operas. He wrote the Messiah an Oratorio that we will discuss.

The Messiah is in three parts part 1 is the Christmas section then goes to easter in part 2 and ends with part 3 which tells of the redemption of the world through faith. It is sung with a 4 part chorus and orchestra. It is Through-composed but with a recurring refrain and the texture is a mix of everything.
